Embodied Multimedia: When Multimodal Signal Processing Meets Embodied Intelligence

Co-located with the IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo 2026
5 July - 9 July 2025, Bangkok, Thailand

overview

Embodied Intelligence is redefining AI by shifting focus from abstract reasoning to learning through physical interaction. In embodied systems, multimedia serves as the essential medium for perception, communication, and action, enabling intelligent agents to see, hear, sense, and act within dynamic environments. Because embodied AI creates closed-loop “Perception-Decision-Action” structures, the behavior of such systems emerges from continuous environmental engagement rather than static dataset processing.

Our workshop brings together researchers from signal processing, computer vision, robotics, and network communications to address critical challenges in building next-generation intelligent systems. We propose a Five-Layer Unified Architecture encompassing:

  • Active data acquisition with semantic-preserving compression.
  • Task-oriented semantic communication.
  • World models for cognitive reasoning.
  • Immersive VR/AR interaction.
  • Physiological signal-based evaluation.

With spatial computing devices and humanoid robots becoming reality, the time is ripe for establishing Embodied Multimedia as a coherent research agenda.

Topics and Areas

We invite submissions on topics including but not limited to:

  • Active perception and viewpoint planning.
  • Multi-sensor fusion strategies for embodied agents.
  • Task-oriented E2E compression for embodied systems.
  • Semantic communication for embodied tasks.
  • Edge-cloud collaborative computing for multimedia.
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation for embodied agents.
  • Cross-modal retrieval in dynamic environments.
  • World models and predictive learning.
  • Embodied generation and environment creation.
  • High-fidelity avatar generation and motion synthesis.
  • Human-avatar interaction and motion capture.
  • VR/AR content rendering and display technologies.
  • Haptic rendering and multimodal feedback systems.
  • Physiological signal-based user experience evaluation.
  • Novel metrics for immersion and interaction comfort.
  • Adaptive optimization based on real-time user feedback.
